DISCIPLINARY COMMITTEE TRIBUNAL ORDERS

Wayne Pulman, 11 Axis Ct, Mallard Way, Swansea Vale, Swansea SA7 0AJ

Complaint: On dates between February 2010 and February 2012, he conspired with others to make untrue or misleading representations to “A”, namely that fee funding in respect of a credit agreement for the provision of services by “B Ltd” related to genuine documents, duly authorised. He knowingly remained party to the carrying on of B Ltd’s business for a fraudulent purpose and he conspired with others to steal funds held in accounts for the credit of “C Ltd” and B Ltd. He was later found guilty of three criminal offences including conspiracy to commit fraud, fraudulent trading and theft, and sentenced to three years, six months in prison.

Order: Exclusion, £3,300 costs.

 

Dhananjay Pandya, Porch House, Little Raveley, Huntingdon PE28 2NQ

Complaint: On 18 and 19 July 2015, Mr DR Pandya FCA assaulted his wife and was convicted of two offences of common assault at Huntingdon Magistrates Court.

Order: Exclusion, £3,261.50 costs.

 

Sean Fox, Flat 3, Pear Tree Ct, Turners Place, Holmer Green, High Wycombe HP15 6RL

Complaint: He advised his client on 2 December 2013, 12 December 2013 and 16 January 2014 that he had submitted the accounts for “A LLP” for the year ended 31 March 2013 to Companies House when he knew that this was incorrect. He also advised his client on 18 July 2014 and 6 August 2014 that he had submitted A LLP’s accounts for the year ended 31 March 2014 to Companies House when he knew this was incorrect. Furthermore, he failed to prepare and submit to HMRC tax returns not just for A LLP for the tax year 2013/14 but for two clients as well.

Order: Severe reprimand, £4,000 fine, £3,300 costs.

 

Sean Fox, Flat 3, Pear Tree Ct, Turners Pl, Holmer Green, High Wycombe HP15 6RL

Complaint: He failed to submit to Companies House the accounts of “C Ltd” for the year ended 30 June 2014 when he had been instructed to do so. He failed to prepare and submit to HMRC C Ltd’s corporation tax return for the year ended 30 June 2014 which was due by 30 June 2015, and which he had been instructed to file. And he failed to respond to seven separate requests for him to return C Ltd’s books and records.

Order: Severe reprimand, £2,600 fine, £5,500 costs

Mohan Kripalani, 73 High St, Hampton Hill, Hampton, Middlesex TW12 1NH

Complaint: He arranged a subsale SDLT planning scheme for a client but failed to warn him specifically of a risk that the scheme could be rendered ineffective due to proposed retrospective legislation. He failed to accurately complete his firm’s 2010-2014 ICAEW annual returns because he did not declare a connected entity of the firm. Contrary to disciplinary byelaw 11.1e, he also failed to ensure that all new clients were informed in writing of the name of the principal to be contacted if they wished to make a complaint and the client’s right to complain to ICAEW.

Order: Reprimand, £3,750 fine, £3,688 costs.

 

Mohan Kripalani, 73 High St, Hampton Hill, Hampton, Middlesex TW12 1NH
