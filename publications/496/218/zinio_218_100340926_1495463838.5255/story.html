By Joel Mathis

CHARGE FORCE

We’ve often praised the toughness of Otterbox’s cases for iPhone and iPad. Now they’ve teamed up with battery-maker Mophie to create Otterbox-compatible products: The Charge Force Battery ($69.95) adds 39 hours of talk time to your phone and fits Otterbox’s uniVERSE case, while and the Charge Force Adapter ($49.95) turns the case into a wireless charging case. Now Otterbox cases can be tough and powerful.

SLXTREME 7

This company has teamed up with Mossy Oak to offer the SLXtreme 7 in Break-Up Country camo ($150) a camouflage-themed version of its rugged solar-powered iPhone case. It includes a 4,000-mAh battery, can survive a two-meter drop, and is waterproof up to two meters deep. (Of course, it’s camouflaged: If you drop it in the forest, you might never find it again.)

FLIP

The Flip stylus ($80) is a “smart” device that doesn’t need batteries or a Bluetooth connection to do its work: The stylus is equipped with magnets that work in conjunction with your tablet to determine the location and orientation of the stylus, enabling palm rejection, flip to erase, and other features. It comes with interchangeable tips to let you create precisely the writing, drawing, or painting you choose on your iPad.

RA200 AIRPODS EARHOOKS

Want to make sure your Airpods don’t fall out of your ears and disappear forever? RA200 Airpods Earhooks ($10; $11 on Amazon) help keep your Airpods secure and anchored in your ear; they’re made of premium silicone, and come in two sizes so you get the perfect fit.

HOOVER CAMERA PASSPORT DRONE

The Hover Camera Passport Drone ($500) is a flying camera that broadcasts live video directly to your iPhone or iPad. It’s simple to use and loyal like a puppy dog: Tap the power button, release the drone, and Passport hovers steadily in place, following you and your iOS device wherever you go.

SLINGSTUDIO

Want to put together a pro-style video broadcast on Facebook? SlingStudio ($999) creates a private video-grade wireless network which allows multiple devices—both professional cameras and smartphones— to synchronize and broadcast live video in HD. And if you have the app, you can run the entire operation from your iPad, choosing which camera angle viewers see as an event breaks in real time.
