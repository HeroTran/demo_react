By Joel Mathis

THE DASH PRO

The Dash Pro ($329) is a set of Bluetooth earphones with a unique twist: You control the device via your head gestures. It has in-ear biometric sensors that measure your steps and cadence if you’re walking, pool lengths and breaths if you’re swimming. The Dash Pro also offers iTranslate integration, letting you communicate in up to 40 languages. Want them custom-fitted to your ears? That’ll cost $499.

SWARM BOX

Here’s an accessory designed pretty much for business events: You plug the SwarmBox into a projector, then let meeting attendees log in via their iPhone or iPad. After that, use the device to get instant feedback from attendees in the form of polling, opinion surveys, or even quizzes, with results instantly available. Other features include the Picture Wall to allow the display of idea sketches, and a live blog to allow participants to comment on or ask questions of the presenter. Contact SwarmWorks to get a quote on the system price.

GARMIN VIRB 360

Garmin VIRB 360 ($800) is “a compact, rugged, and fully spherical 360-degree camera” designed for capturing spectacular outdoor scenes. It’s waterproof, comes with its own tripod-handgrip, has four built-in microphones—and it can all be controlled from your iPhone using the Garmin VIRB app.

EVE MOTION

New iOS-controlled products from Elgato that work with the Apple HomeKit are the Eve Motion ($49.95; same price on Amazon), a wireless motion detector (pictured) that sends alerts to your phone whenever someone enters or approaches your home; and the EVE Light Switch ($49.95; $44 on Amazon), a, well, light switch. But it’s a light switch from the future!

iMICROSCOPE R

Here’s a cool Kickstarter project: iMicroscope R (starts at a $59 pledge) turns your phone—as you may have guessed from the name—into a microscope, letting you view the tiniest things in the world up close and personal.

PENNA RETRO BLUETOOTH KEYBOARD

We can’t help but be a bit delighted at the sight of the Penna Retro Bluetooth Keyboard (starts at $114, with pledge through Indiegogo). This typewriter-style keyboard comes with either diamond-style keycaps or retro chrome keycaps— the macro bar on the left is even functional. While it’s functional, we just think it looks really cool. So do crowdfunders, who raised nearly 10 times the sum the makers needed to go into production.
