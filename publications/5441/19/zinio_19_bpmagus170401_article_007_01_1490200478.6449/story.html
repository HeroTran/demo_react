AGE: 37
DIAGNOSIS: BIPOLAR I
YEAR DIAGNOSED: 2007
LOCATION: CHICAGO, IL
OCCUPATION: FREELANCE WRITER

 

*  *  *  *  *

 

FAVORITE BOOK: A Clockwork Orange, by Anthony Burgess. I used to be a punk rocker and that antiestablishment ethos will always remain with me (though not to the violent extent of the book).

FAVORITE FOOD: Lasagna. I make a killer five-cheese version with all fresh ingredients and herbs. The goat cheese really makes it sing.

TO DESTRESS I: Listen to music. I calm my nerves and slow my racing thoughts with albums like Air’s Moon Safari and Joni Mitchell’s Blue and playlists of Beethoven, Bach, Mozart, and Tchaikovsky.

MOST TREASURED ITEM: My 1994 Fender Stratocaster guitar. I got it when I was 14. It’s got that ’90s grunge sound everyone covets now.

IT MIGHT SURPRISE YOU TO KNOW: I spent many summers at tennis camp growing up. I abhor sports, but I do play tennis.

I WISH I WERE BETTER AT: Public speaking. I’m terrified of it. I just need to practice.

BIGGEST ADVENTURE SO FAR: I went to Bali several years ago. A highlight was attending a Balinese funeral, an upbeat celebration that parades through the streets.

BEST RESOURCE: My dad. He was a stay-at-home dad in the ’80s—avant-garde for the time—and I’ve learned so much from him. He is a wellspring of advice.

BIGGEST CHALLENGE I’VE OVERCOME: Emerging from a deep depression in 2008. I was crying every day for a year and constantly asking for hugs from friends and family. Finally my doctor and I found the right medications to stabilize me.

PROUDEST ACHIEVEMENT: When I was a producer for MTV News, I orchestrated a three-part series about the future of the music industry.

GREATEST LESSON I’VE LEARNED: Don’t be afraid to be different.
