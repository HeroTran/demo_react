All clear

Clutter in our living spaces can send our stress levels through the roof. Trying to decide what to give up, though, can be just as frustrating.

Take the pain out of decluttering by taking these tips from certified feng shui consultant Marianne Gordon:

  1. Fill a garbage bag with items to donate. If you keep thinking about something in the bag, remove it the next day. To experience the immediate relief of decluttering, drop everything else off at a donation bank within two to three days.
  2. Scan your rooms. Does that coffee table knick-knack complement your décor, or did you buy it because it was on sale? If the latter, discard.
  3. Get rid of any mementos associated with less-than-happy memories.

 

 

A sound transition

Spring brings with it more daylight—and, with that, a greater temptation to stay up later.

To maintain a healthy sleep schedule during this seasonal change, invest in blackout shades or curtains and draw them several hours before bedtime to cue your body to settle down.

If you suffer from springtime allergies, using a neti pot or another nasal rinse can combat allergens that disrupt breathing patterns and threaten a solid slumber.

For evening snacks, choose foods that contain tryptophan and other sleep-enhancing substances. Some go-to options: walnuts, almonds, bananas, hummus and cheese.

 

 

Lean in (and hug)

We’re often told to “lean in” to work—to push harder, to achieve more. Instead, psychiatrist Robert Waldinger thinks we should lean in to relationships.

That means less Facebook and more face time. Letting go of grudges. Perking up stale friendships with fresh activities.

“Giving people our full, undivided attention is probably the most valuable thing we have to offer,” Waldinger says.

He should know: Waldinger is the current director of the Harvard Study of Adult Development, which, in tracking participants over a whopping 75 years, has found that those with strong ties are both happier and healthier.
