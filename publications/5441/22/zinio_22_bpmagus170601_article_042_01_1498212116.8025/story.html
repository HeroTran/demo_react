D - fense

With summer here, it’s a great time to get your daily dose of Vitamin D from the sun.

But how much ray time do you need to meet your “D” needs? Experts say that 10-15 minutes of sun exposure on your bare skin does the trick for most of us.

You can also supplement with vitamins and through your diet, eating foods such as salmon, tuna, eggs and fortified milk.

The sunshine vitamin has myriad benefits—including helping us maintain healthy bones and teeth—so consult your doctor if you’re concerned you’re not getting enough.

 

Talking temps

If a sound slumber eludes you, here’s a way to fake your body into a sleep cycle:

Take a hot shower, then get into a cool bed. The drop in your body’s temperature naturally mimics day and night, and may help guide you to sleep.

 

Notifications no more

“In the contemporary struggle for concentration, notifications aren’t just low-hanging fruit; they’re fruit-that long ago fell to the ground and now lie rotting.... To live with frequent notifications is to outsource decisions about the allocation of your attention to a vast committee of friends, colleagues and strangers, most of whom have no incentive to put your interests first: they want an answer to their email right now - or more engagement for their app.”

—Oliver Burkeman, columnist for the Guardian, calling on us to enjoy fewer distractions by turning off our phone’s email, social media and app-generated notifications.

 

Count your blessings

Science has shown that an attitude of gratitude can make us happier, but a recent study shows that expressing it face-to-face is more beneficial still.

Psychologists at the University of Limerick found that participants who kept a gratitude journal and then, each week, offered an in-person thanks to someone in it fared better than participants who only wrote in a gratitude journal or those who journaled about their day.

Specifically, the gratitude-expressing group experienced the biggest decrease in negative emotions while feeling less depressed and more emotionally balanced.
