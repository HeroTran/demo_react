Q: HOW DO I GET RID OF THE “PICK UP WHERE YOU LEFT OFF?” PROMPT IN MICROSOFT WORD?

A: When you reopen a document in modern versions of Microsoft Word, it displays an offer to “Pick up where you left off,” as if Word were an e-reader of some kind. I tend to open a document and then immediately hit some keys—perhaps Alt+W, E to view in draft mode, or Ctrl+F to find something. These commands are ignored because of the “Pick up...” offer. It’s seriously annoying. And I finally figured out how to kill it!

Launch REGEDIT and navigate to the key HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Office\16.0\Word\Reading Locations. Delete every single subkey; each of them is the where-you-left-off information for a different document.

Now right-click the Reading Locations key and choose Permissions. Click the button labeled Advanced. Near the bottom of the resulting window you’ll find a button labeled Disable inheritance. Click that, and click OK repeatedly. You’ve ensured that Word can’t create any more of those remembered pick-up points.

If at some point you want to re-enable that feature, just follow the same instructions, but click the Enable inheritance button.

—Neil Rubenking, PCMag lead analyst for security
