BY PCMAG STAFF

MICROSOFT PROJECT SCORPIO

Ready for the future of gaming? This new console—due to ship in Q4—looks to be the most powerful performer ever. Specs include a 6-teraflop GPU, which enables 4K and mixed-reality gaming. On the memory front, Project Scorpio will deliver a total bandwidth of 326GB/s, achieved by using 12 1GB GDDR5 memory chips running on a 384-bit bus.

Price not yet announced, www.xbox.com.com

ASTROHAUS FREEWRITE

This very cool connected typewriter is made for the serious writer who doesn’t want to be tempted by internet distractions. The Freewrite’s mechanical keyboard feels and sounds like old-fashioned typewriter keyboards. Words that you type appear on a backlit e-ink display that sits above the keyboard and via Wi-Fi, sync immediately with Dropbox, Google Drive, or Evernote.

$499.00, www.getfreewrite.com

GRYPHON

Not just another a Wi-Fi router, the Gryphon promises to protect your family and their devices from Internet threats with “24/7 intelligent intrusion detection [in beta] and active whole house malware filtering.” Also built in are numerous parental-control features: Through its app, you can view your connected devices, manage your kids’ site access, and review their browsing history.

$179 and up (preorder), www.indiegogo.com
